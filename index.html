Best Potato Salad

"Maybe not the best, but it's up there."

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs gold potatoes
  • 3 Tbsp dill pickle juice (total)
  • 1 Tbsp yellow mustard
  • 2 dill pickles finely minced
  • 1/2 red onion finely minced
  • 1 stalk celery finely minced
  • 1/2 - 1 Tsp kosher salt
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise (ideally Hellmans, and preferably not light)
  • 1/4 cup full fat 14% sour cream
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Peel and dice the potatoes into roughly 3/4 inch pieces.
  2. Boil until tender in well salted water. Drain.
  3. Mix 2 Tbsp pickle juice with the yellow mustard. While the potatoes are still warm, spread the potatoes on a cookie sheet and drizzle the pickle juice/mustard mixture overtop. Stir gently to coat all the potatoes.
  4. Refrigerate around 30 minutes.
  5. Combine all other ingredients except the eggs in a bowl large enough to contain the potato salad. Don't forget the third Tbsp of pickle juice.
  6. Fold in the potatoes and then the eggs gently and chill for around one hour. Serve and enjoy.

I found this recipe on Glebe Kitchen. It's a modified version of an older recipe from America's Test Kitchen. It's my favorite side dish to make, and I usually prepare it in advance and divvy out portions for lunch. I make it once every two weeks, or so. The effort-to-result ratio is pretty good; it's simple, easy, and only somewhat labor intensive. It tastes great, relative to the amount of work put in. It doesn't require too much babysitting, beyond preparing the potatoes. I make this thing in large quantities, and in our household, it lasts the better part of a few days.

Notes

  • Glebe Kitchen's recipe calls for 2 finely minced regular-sized dill pickles. I replace them with 5 finely minced mini dill gherkins for my mine, since that's what I usually have on hand.
